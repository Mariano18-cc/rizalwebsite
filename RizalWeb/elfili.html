El Filibusterismo Summary

Introduction

Set 13 years after the events of *Noli Me Tangere*, *El Filibusterismo* depicts the return of Crisóstomo Ibarra, now disguised as Simoun, a wealthy jeweler, seeking revenge against the Spanish colonial government.

Plot Highlights

Simoun uses his wealth and influence to encourage corruption among government officials, intending to incite public outrage and rebellion. He allies with revolutionary groups to plan an uprising during a grand wedding reception.

However, Simoun's plans fail due to betrayal, and he is forced to flee. In his final moments, he confesses his true identity to a priest and seeks redemption before his death.

Main Characters

Simoun: The protagonist, formerly Crisóstomo Ibarra, now a vengeful jeweler.
Basilio: A medical student and Sisa's surviving son, involved in Simoun’s revolution.
Isagani: Basilio's idealistic friend, known for his patriotism and sacrifice.
Kabesa Tales: A farmer who loses his land to friars, symbolizing the plight of Filipino peasants.
Padre Florentino: A Filipino priest who represents moral integrity and nationalism.

About the Author

José Rizal (1861–1896), a Filipino nationalist and revolutionary, wrote *El Filibusterismo* as a sequel to *Noli Me Tangere*. His works exposed the abuses of Spanish colonial rule, inspiring Filipino nationalism and the struggle for independence.
